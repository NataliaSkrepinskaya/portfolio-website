Website Puella Present Decor

The Task

Puella is web page, which was born from an idea of one woman passionate about handmade gifts and decoration for interiors. She has an Instagram account @presents_decor, but she wanted more. A web page would give her an opportunity to sell gifts and decoration as easy and enjoyable as possible.

My goal was to develop the most convenient and simple responsive design for a website. The target audience would be people age 18 to 65+ years old. Users can browse handmade collections, choose products online, and have the ability to contact the seller directly.

Affinity Map

To achieve this goal, I conducted quantitative and qualitative research, focusing on surveys and interviews.

User Research Analysis

After conducting surveys, interviews and creating affinity map for Puella website I presented my conclusions to the stakeholder:
People have different motivations for giving a present: holidays, birthdays, just-because, expected gifts;
The pain point in process of presenting: negative emotions of the receiver;
The age criteria for buying a gift: 10 years plus;
Interior decoration service can also be used for a wedding;
The process of presenting flowers does not look meticulous or hard. Usually, it happens at the last moment (buying near my house, metro station, nearest shop), it happens when someone didnâ€™t come up with something more interesting;
The process of finding, purchasing, and presenting handmade gifts feels harder and longer. But it is more personal, exclusive and fashionable.

User Persona

I created 3 user personas based on surveys and interviews. I created 2 different female and one male user personas because of the most quantity of persons who by presents or flowers via the internet are females. Conversely, male prefer to buy flowers in shops near their office, home or along the way to the party.

User Flows

Based on created user persona I developed the user flows. Exactly this web page will have new clients so they must understand clearly for what this service, the logo and home page must describe clearly the main offer of the owner. In my opinion, the navigation must show few important functions (as presents, bouquets and contacts) on mobile devices, other could be hidden in the hamburger menu. Exactly all services must be clear describing as different payment methods, delivery methods, possibility to change ingredients of bouquets.

Rapid Prototype for Mobile

Prototyping with pen and paper is very useful and cheap method. When I used paper prototypes in InVision for usability testing I received more relevant feedback from the target audience unlike I used mid- or -high fidelity prototypes in InVision. Because when users see the high fidelity prototypes they think that everything must be clickable and they pay attention more on this rather than on real usability problems.

Low-Fidelity Prototype for Mobile

On opinion of my stakeholder, the low-fidelity prototypes were less understandable than paper prototypes. Because I created it by eyes of a designer not by eyes of a user. By the way, first I might add the text for all boxes and text fields, the second I might indicate the icons as signs not as rectangles. In other words, low-fid prototypes must be understandable for stakeholder also.

High-Fidelity Prototype for Mobile

Developing high-fid prototype I focused on Menu as I said before that it will include 3 main functions and others will be hidden in a hamburger menu (on mobile devices). I added free consultation is one of the important functions for users ( I understood it during the interviews). The Home Page must include CTA button for quick connection. Real feedback from real people it works very good for the attracting new clients.
